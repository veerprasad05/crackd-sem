## User Study 3

### User Information
- Relationship to the user: friend
- Whether the user has used your app before: none
- Whether the user has experience with similar applications: none

### Observation Context
- Where the study took place: food hall
- What device was used: laptop
- What instructions you gave the user: no instructions other than free exploration
- What tasks you asked the user to perform: free exploration

### Three Things the User Liked (Functionality Only)
1. Easy to upload pictures
2. Captions generated were funny and resonated with a “real” sense of humor
3. Captions list had lots of variety, easy to scroll and resonate with what is funny
4. Easy to like/dislike and sort content
5. User experience was engaging and fun; loved the colors and button features

### Three Areas for Improvement
1. Some friction scrolling through different pages, especially jumping from page 1 to 5
2. Only 1-2 photos per page can feel repetitive until filtering to featured/sorted differently
3. Ensure pagination and image density are easy to control for users looking for faster exploration

### Observed Friction or Confusion
- Some hesitation while navigating the page controls and jumping across multiple pages.
- User noted repetitiveness of images when pagination size is low.

### Behavioral Observations
- User scrolled through the caption list and interacted with like/dislike controls frequently.
- Explored filter settings and noticed better results with featured-only/sorting options.

### User Quotes
"It’s easy to upload pictures and the captions feel real and funny." 
"I want to jump from page 1 to 5 faster without multiple page clicks."

### Things I learned from observing users
- They value dynamic design (animated welcome and clean minimal UI) and functional controls (filters, sort, like/dislike, image upload).
- Consistency in navigation is essential: non-clickable branding text or missing home behavior causes confusion.
- Users evaluate experience through content density; they notice repetitive photos and pagination friction quickly.

### Things I found surprising
- All three users gave strong positive comments on ease and UI vibe, even while finding concrete usability gaps.
- Two users specifically noticed the first load and layout; one called the first screen empty, one loved the color/buttons but felt pagination was awkward.
- They intuitively reach for expected behavior (app title should link home, quick page jump, 3+ images per page for variety).

### Patterns noticed across multiple users
- Strong pattern: “easy to use” and “fun” vs. friction areas focused on navigation and content flow (voting, pagination, repeated images).
- All users used filtering or sorting and found it valuable; they want more control over what appears and in what order.
- All users were not stuck on joke quality, confirming the requirement to focus on functionality/usability.

### At least three specific improvements I plan to make
1. Fix votes consistency with filters so rating updates persist without reset or conflict.
2. Make top navigation clearly clickable (brand text to home, explicit home button) and improve initial-screen content density.
3. Add pagination usability enhancements (jump-to-page input, continuous scroll, and configurable items per page) and reduce repetition by grouping or deduping near duplicates.
4. Add a text label atop image groups and include better on-page info so users can search and skim (e.g., “Funny Family”, “Work Meme”).
5. Adjust capitalization presentation per user preferences and streamline first screen engagement (quick-start actions, featured thumbnails).
